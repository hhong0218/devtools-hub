What is HTML entity encoding?

      
HTML has a handful of characters with special meaning — chiefly <, >, and &. If you want those characters to appear as literal text rather than be parsed as markup, you replace them with HTML entities such as &lt;, &gt;, and &amp;. Encoding (escaping) turns raw characters into entities; decoding (unescaping) turns them back. This tool does both, entirely in your browser.

How to use it

      
1. Pick Encode or Decode. 2. For encoding, choose whether to escape only the markup-critical characters (recommended) or every non-ASCII character too. 3. Paste your text and click Convert, then Copy the result.


      
Is my data safe?

      
Yes. This encoder runs entirely in your browser using plain JavaScript and the built-in DOM parser — no external libraries and no network requests. Nothing you paste is ever sent to a server.
